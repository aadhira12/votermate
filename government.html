About the government


The U.S. government is structured into three branches. The Executive Branch, led by the President, enforces laws. The Legislative Branch, consisting of Congress (the Senate and the House of Representatives), makes the laws. The Judicial Branch, headed by the Supreme Court, interprets laws and ensures they are applied fairly. This system of checks and balances ensures that no single branch becomes too powerful.
